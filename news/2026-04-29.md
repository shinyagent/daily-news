## AI NEWS

**1. GPT-5.5 is OpenAI's Most Capable Agentic AI Model Yet**
OpenAI launched GPT-5.5 on April 23 as what it calls "a new class of intelligence for real work and powering agents," built from the ground up to plan, use tools, check its own output, and work through tasks independently. The model scores 82.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 (vs Claude Opus 4.7's 69.4%) and 58.6% on SWE-Bench Pro for GitHub issue resolution, though it trails Claude on MCP Atlas tool-use benchmarks. API access costs $5/$30 per million input/output tokens — twice GPT-5.4's rate — with OpenAI claiming 20% higher effective efficiency making the premium worthwhile for agentic workloads.
Source: https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/gpt-5-5-is-openais-most-capable-agentic-ai-model-yet-at-twice-the-api-price/

**2. IDC: EMEA CIOs Struggle to Move AI Pilots Into Production**
New IDC research reveals that only 9% of EMEA organisations have delivered quantifiable business outcomes from most of their AI projects over the past two years, with the remaining 91% stuck in pilot phase. Boards are slowing, scaling back, or refocusing initiatives due to execution issues and financial validation pressures rather than any loss of technical interest. The fix requires rewriting ROI calculations to capture indirect value — preventing failures rather than headcount reduction — and building governance structures early rather than treating compliance as a restriction.
Source: https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/idc-how-emea-cios-can-jumpstart-ai-rollouts/

**3. IBM Launches AI Platform Bob to Regulate SDLC Costs**
IBM is launching Bob, an AI-first development platform designed to anchor enterprise engineering and rein in software delivery costs driven by accumulated technical debt, hybrid cloud complexity, and rigid compliance requirements. The platform maps legacy dependencies before initiating code refactoring, routes tasks dynamically across multiple AI models (including Claude, Mistral, and IBM Granite), and embeds guardrails for prompt normalisation, sensitive data scanning, and real-time policy enforcement. Over 80,000 IBM employees now use the platform internally, reporting a 45% average productivity gain; external clients like Cloud Solutions provider Blue Pearl compressed a standard 30-day Java upgrade into three days.
Source: https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/ibm-launches-ai-platform-bob-to-regulate-sdlc-costs/

## TRAVEL TECH NEWS

**1. Investors Bet on 'OTA 2.0' — But Builders Aren't There Yet**
Investors at Skift Asia Forum see AI creating a new generation of travel distributors, but panellists acknowledged that the real operational work still runs through established OTAs. The discussion highlighted a gap between the investor thesis for AI-driven disruption and the practical reality that legacy infrastructure and trust relationships are not easily displaced. The tension between building for the future and operating in the present dominated the conversation.
Source: https://skift.com/2026/04/29/skift-asia-forum-ai-panel-otas-trust/

**2. Booking.com: Next-Gen Travellers Want Safety and Price More Than the Experiences They Say They Want**
New research from Booking.com presented at Skift Asia Forum found that travel demand in Asia is holding up, but travellers are more price-sensitive and safety-conscious than their expressed preferences suggest. The gap between declared desires and actual booking behaviour has significant implications for how travel brands should market and package offerings — particularly for younger demographics who say they prioritse experiences but consistently choose lower-cost options.
Source: https://skift.com/2026/04/29/booking-com-what-the-next-generation-of-travelers-want-and-will-pay-for/

**3. Trip.com and H World Face the Limits of Their China Playbook Abroad**
Trip.com and H World Group executives discussed at Skift Asia Forum the challenge of global expansion when the playbook that drove success in China — state partnerships, super-app integration, high-touch service models — doesn't translate directly to other markets. Both companies are working through which elements of their domestic model to keep, adapt, or abandon as they pursue international growth, with cultural adaptation and regulatory patchwork cited as the biggest friction points.
Source: https://skift.com/2026/04/29/trip-com-h-world-international-strategy/

**4. Prism's G6 Launches Studio 6 Plus Budget Brand in the U.S.**
Prism Group, led by CEO Ritesh Agarwal, is launching Studio 6 Plus as an extended-stay budget brand aimed at upmarket positioning without abandoning the value segment. The move reflects ongoing consolidation in the U.S. extended-stay hotel market, where operators are seeking sharper franchise terms to attract developers amid rising construction costs. Studio 6 Plus is designed to appeal to franchisees looking for better economics than competitors like OYO offer.
Source: https://skift.com/2026/04/29/prisms-g6-debuts-new-budget-brand-in-the-u-s-studio-6-plus/

**5. Accor's Andrew Langdon on Branded Hotel Shift: It's Structural, Not Cyclical**
Accor's Andrew Langdon told Skift Asia Forum that the shift toward branded hotels in Asia is a structural outcome of market maturity and generational change, not merely a cyclical response to cost pressures or timing. Branded hotels provide consistent quality assurances that independent operators struggle to match as travel becomes more mobile and digitally mediated. The framing suggests major international chains are likely to gain share in Asia for the foreseeable future.
Source: https://skift.com/2026/04/29/accor-on-hotel-competition-at-skift-asia-forum-2026/

**6. Allianz Partners on Customer Loyalty: Stop Trying to 'Own' the Relationship**
Allianz Partners' Vinay Surana told Skift Asia Forum that travel brands should abandon the goal of "owning" the customer relationship and instead focus on being indispensable at the moments that matter. The pushback against brand-centrism reflects a broader recognition that travellers move across multiple platforms and channels, and that loyalty built through utility outperforms loyalty built through lock-in. The framing has implications for how travel companies think about loyalty programmes and partnerships.
Source: https://skift.com/2026/04/29/allianz-partners-on-customer-loyalty-at-skift-asia-forum-2026/
